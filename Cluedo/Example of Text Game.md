```
Miss Scarlett (Player 1)
Colonel Mustard (Player 2)
Mrs White (Player 3)
Mr Green (Player 4)
Mrs Peacock (Player 5)
Professor Plum (Player 6)



Three cards have been randomly chosen to be the Murder Circumstances, and will be hidden in an envelope. Your mission is to work out which cards have been chosen. The rest of the cards have been dealt into your hands.

Your goal is to move around the board making suggestions until you’ve worked out which cards your opponents don’t have, ie. the Murder Circumstances. 

For each turn, two dice will be rolled, and the total number being the number of moves you have. You will then be able to move the corresponding squares to a desired spot on the grid.

A suggestion can be made once you’ve moved into a room. It comprises of the room you are in, a weapon and a character. Once it is made, each player, in a clockwise fashion, attempts to prove that the suggestion isn’t possible. They can do this by showing one card to you that matches either the room, weapon or character. If a player has more than one card that matches the suggestion, they can choose which to show. Once a card has been shown to you, your turn is over. If you made a suggestion in that room your last turn, you have to move to a different room.

Once you have worked out which cards are the Murder Circumstances (or have a good idea) you can make an accusation. Similar to a suggestion, instead of checking the player’s hands the Murder Circumstances are checked. If you are correct, you’ve won. If you are wrong, you are excluded from the game except to prove other player’s suggestions wrong.

Please create a Detective Card for yourself by writing down all the possible Murder Circumstances:

Characters: Miss Scarlett, Colonel Mustard, Mrs White, Mr Green, Mrs Peacock, Professor Plum

Weapons: Candlestick, Dagger, Lead Pipe, Revolver, Rope, Spanner

Rooms: Kitchen, Ballroom, Conservatory, Billard Room, Library, Study, Hall, Lounge, Dining Room

This can be used to indicate which Circumstances you know aren’t the Murder Circumstances (ie. cards in your hand or cards in other players hands)


You can view the rules (1), view the circumstances (2), or continue (3).

Please choose a number.
 ```
